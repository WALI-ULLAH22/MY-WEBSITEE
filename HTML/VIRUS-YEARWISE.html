Selected Virus Chronologies

Yellow Fever

  • 1901 — Yellow fever virus characterized; major outbreaks across Africa and the Americas.
  • 20th century — Vaccine developed and used for mass immunization.

Spanish Flu (Influenza H1N1)

  • 1918 — Devastating H1N1 influenza pandemic with very high mortality.
  • Subsequent decades — Influenza surveillance and vaccine development.

Ebola

  • 1976 — First recognized outbreaks in Sudan and the DRC (Zaire).
  • 2014–2016 — Major West Africa epidemic with international response.

HIV / AIDS

  • 1981 — First clinical cases recognized; later identified as HIV, leading to a global pandemic.
  • 1990s–present — Antiretroviral therapy dramatically improved outcomes.

Smallpox

  • Ancient — Smallpox recorded in historical texts for millennia.
  • 1980 — Smallpox declared eradicated after a global vaccination campaign.

SARS

  • 2003 — Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome emerged and spread internationally, prompting improved outbreak controls.

H1N1 (2009)

  • 2009 — Novel H1N1 influenza emerged and caused a WHO-declared pandemic.

MERS-CoV

  • 2012 — Middle East Respiratory Syndrome first identified in Saudi Arabia; sporadic outbreaks since.

Zika

  • 1947 — Zika virus first identified in Uganda; 2015–2016 epidemic in the Americas linked to congenital effects.

COVID-19

  • 2019 — SARS-CoV-2 identified in Wuhan; pandemic declared in 2020.
  • 2020–2024 — Global vaccination campaigns, treatment advances, and variant monitoring.
