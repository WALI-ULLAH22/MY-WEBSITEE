Zika Virus

Zika virus is primarily transmitted by Aedes mosquitoes. While most infections are mild, infection during pregnancy can cause severe birth defects including microcephaly. Prevention emphasizes mosquito control and protection for pregnant people.

  • Transmission: Aedes mosquito bites, sexual transmission, mother-to-child
  • Key risk: Congenital Zika syndrome when infection occurs during pregnancy
  • Treatment: Supportive care; no widely available vaccine as of now

Diagnosis & Clinical Care

Diagnosis uses RT-PCR during the acute window and serology later. Clinical care is supportive; special monitoring and fetal assessment are indicated for pregnant women with possible exposure or infection.

Prevention

Prevention focuses on reducing mosquito exposure (repellents, clothing, bed nets, source reduction) and practicing safer sex or abstaining during and after travel to affected areas for those planning pregnancy.

Global Statistics & Impact

Reported Cases: 500,000+ cases during major outbreaks

Zika gained global attention during outbreaks in 2015–2016, particularly due to its effects on newborn health.
