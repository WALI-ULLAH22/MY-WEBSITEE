Influenza (Flu)

Influenza, commonly known as the flu, is a contagious viral infection caused by influenza A, B, and C viruses. It mainly infects the nose, throat, and lungs and spreads easily through airborne droplets when an infected person coughs or sneezes. The virus can also spread by touching contaminated surfaces and then touching the face.

Common flu symptoms include high fever, chills, muscle and body aches, headache, sore throat, runny or blocked nose, cough, and extreme tiredness. Most people recover within one to two weeks, but influenza can be very dangerous for older adults, young children, pregnant women, and people with weakened immune systems.

Transmission

Influenza spreads mainly through respiratory droplets when infected people cough or sneeze, and by touching contaminated surfaces then touching the face. Seasonal outbreaks occur because influenza viruses (especially types A and B) rapidly mutate, leading to antigenic drift and occasional antigenic shift.

Symptoms

Common symptoms include sudden onset of fever, chills, muscle aches, headache, sore throat, cough, nasal congestion, and fatigue. Most cases resolve within one to two weeks, but complications like pneumonia, exacerbation of chronic conditions, and secondary bacterial infections can occur.

Diagnosis & Treatment

Diagnosis is by rapid antigen tests or RT-PCR. Antiviral medications (e.g., oseltamivir, zanamivir, baloxavir) can reduce duration and severity if started early for high-risk patients. Supportive care includes hydration, rest, and fever control.

Prevention & Vaccination

Annual influenza vaccination, targeted to predicted circulating strains, is the most effective prevention. Vaccination reduces transmission, severe disease and hospitalizations. Additional measures include hand hygiene, respiratory etiquette, and staying home when sick.

LATEST UPDATE

Seasonal influenza continues to circulate and public-health authorities recommend annual vaccination, especially for vulnerable groups. Surveillance informs vaccine strain selection each year.

Global Statistics & Impact

Estimated Annual Cases: Around 1 billion cases worldwide

Influenza spreads every year, especially during winter seasons. While most cases are mild, the high number of infections makes it a major public health concern globally.
