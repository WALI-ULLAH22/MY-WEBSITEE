Smallpox Vaccine — Key Information

Overview & History

The smallpox vaccine, based on vaccinia virus, led to the eradication of smallpox in 1980 — a milestone in public-health history. Routine vaccination stopped after eradication; stockpiles remain for emergency preparedness.

Legacy

Smallpox vaccination campaigns demonstrated the power of coordinated global vaccination programs and remain a model for disease elimination efforts.
