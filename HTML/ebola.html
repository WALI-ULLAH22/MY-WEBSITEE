Ebola Virus Disease (EVD)

Ebola disease is a severe viral illness caused by orthoebolaviruses. It spreads through direct contact with infected people or animals and requires rapid public-health action to control outbreaks.

  • Transmission: Direct contact with blood or bodily fluids, contaminated materials, or infected animals
  • Severity: Case fatality rates vary widely (25–90% historically)
  • Prevention: Infection control, safe burials, vaccination and community engagement

Diagnosis & Treatment

Diagnosis is by PCR testing for viral RNA. Treatment centers on high-quality supportive care (fluids, electrolytes, oxygen) and, where available, specific therapeutics such as monoclonal antibodies that have been shown to improve survival.

Prevention & Outbreak Response

Control measures include case identification, isolation, contact tracing, safe infection prevention and control in healthcare settings, safe burial practices and ring vaccination with approved vaccines during outbreaks.

Global Statistics & Impact

Total Recorded Cases: 35,000+ cases worldwide

Ebola outbreaks are rare but severe. Even limited outbreaks can overwhelm healthcare systems due to high fatality rates.
