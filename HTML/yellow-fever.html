Yellow Fever

Yellow fever is a viral hemorrhagic disease transmitted by mosquitoes in parts of Africa and South America. A single dose of an effective vaccine provides long-lasting protection and is the key prevention tool.

  • Transmission: Aedes and Haemagogus mosquitoes
  • Presentation: Ranges from mild febrile illness to severe disease with jaundice and bleeding
  • Prevention: Vaccination and mosquito control

Diagnosis & Treatment

Diagnosis is by serology and molecular testing at reference laboratories. There is no specific antiviral; management is supportive, focusing on intensive care when needed.

Prevention

Vaccination (single dose) gives long-lasting protection and is recommended for people living in or traveling to endemic areas. Mosquito control and surveillance are essential to prevent outbreaks.

Global Statistics & Impact

Estimated Annual Cases: About 200,000 cases

Yellow fever continues to affect regions of Africa and South America. Despite vaccines, outbreaks still occur due to low vaccination coverage in some areas.
