Dengue Vaccine

Several dengue vaccines have been developed to reduce severe disease and hospitalizations in endemic populations. Use is tailored by age and previous exposure because antibody responses to different serotypes can affect risk profiles.

Types & Use

Licensed vaccines (e.g., Dengvaxia in select settings, Qdenga/TAK-003) have specific recommendations regarding serostatus and age. Vaccination programs are implemented based on local epidemiology and WHO guidance.

Safety & Effectiveness

Vaccine effectiveness varies by vaccine and serotype; some products are indicated only for people with prior dengue exposure due to safety considerations. Ongoing post-licensure monitoring guides use.

LATEST UPDATE

Newer dengue vaccines are being introduced in selected endemic countries with tailored policies; vector control remains an essential complement to vaccination.
