HIV & AIDS

Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) attacks the immune system; without treatment it can progress to AIDS. Antiretroviral therapy (ART) enables people living with HIV to live long, healthy lives and prevents onward transmission when viral load is suppressed.

  • Transmission: Body fluids (blood, semen, vaginal fluids, breast milk)
  • Prevention: Condoms, PrEP, harm reduction, safe blood supply, PMTCT
  • Treatment: Lifelong ART; viral suppression prevents sexual transmission (U=U)

Diagnosis & Treatment

HIV is diagnosed with antibody/antigen tests and nucleic acid tests (NAT). After diagnosis, linkage to care and immediate initiation of ART is recommended. Modern regimens are highly effective, safe, and can suppress viral replication to undetectable levels.

Prevention

Prevention includes consistent condom use, testing, ART for people living with HIV, pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP) for high-risk people, harm-reduction for people who inject drugs, and interventions to prevent mother-to-child transmission (PMTCT).

Global Response

Scale-up of testing, ART access and prevention services has transformed HIV from a fatal disease into a manageable chronic condition. International strategies aim to end the HIV epidemic as a public health threat by 2030.

Global Statistics & Impact

Total Infections: Over 85 million people infected globally

HIV remains a long-term global health issue. Although treatments exist, there is no complete cure, and millions continue to live with the virus.
