Prevention Strategies

Prevention reduces transmission, saves lives, and protects health systems. This page summarizes proven prevention methods for major viral diseases.

Vaccination

Vaccines are the most effective prevention tool for many viral diseases. Routine immunization (e.g., hepatitis A/B, yellow fever) and targeted campaigns (e.g., COVID-19 boosters, influenza seasonal vaccines) reduce severe disease and transmission.

Hygiene & Respiratory Measures

  • Regular handwashing and respiratory etiquette (cover coughs/sneezes) help prevent spread.
  • Use well-fitting masks in high-risk settings or during surges for airborne diseases like COVID-19 and influenza.
  • Improve ventilation in indoor spaces and avoid crowded poorly ventilated areas when risk is high.

Vector Control

For mosquito-borne viruses (dengue, Zika, yellow fever): remove standing water, use insecticide-treated nets, apply repellents, and participate in community vector-control programs.

Sexual and Bloodborne Transmission Prevention

Use condoms, practice safer sex, screen blood products, and follow universal precautions in healthcare settings to prevent HIV, hepatitis B and C transmission.

Rabies Prevention

Vaccinate domestic animals, avoid contact with wild animals, and seek immediate medical care after animal bites for wound cleaning and post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP).

Ebola Prevention

Outbreak control requires early detection, isolation of cases, safe burial practices, contact tracing, and ring vaccination of contacts and frontline workers.

Community & Public Health Actions

  • Maintain surveillance and reporting systems to detect outbreaks rapidly.
  • Implement vaccination campaigns and health education to increase uptake.
  • Support healthcare capacity and supply chains for diagnostics, PPE, and therapeutics.
