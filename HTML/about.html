About the WHO Demo

This demo site presents information on viruses, vaccines, prevention and global public health. The content here is for demonstration purposes and mimics WHO-style informational layout.

Mission

To provide leadership on global health matters, shape the research agenda, set norms and standards, and provide technical support to countries.

What we cover

  • Virus information and historical context
  • Vaccine guidance and safety
  • Live map and outbreak response examples

History & Achievements

Since its founding, the WHO has coordinated international responses to infectious disease outbreaks, led eradication campaigns (notably smallpox), and supported the development and distribution of life-saving vaccines. The organization publishes global guidance, supports health systems, and monitors public-health threats.

Programs & Research

WHO runs programs across immunization, emergency response, disease surveillance, and health systems strengthening. Research partnerships support vaccine development, genomic surveillance, and evidence-based policy.

Partnerships

WHO collaborates with UN agencies, national ministries of health, academic institutions, NGOs, and industry partners to improve access to health services and advance public-health goals.
