COVID-19 (Coronavirus Disease 2019)

COVID-19 is a highly contagious infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, which was first identified in late 2019. The virus mainly spreads through respiratory droplets and tiny airborne particles released when an infected person coughs, sneezes, talks, sings, or breathes near others. Because of this, crowded and poorly ventilated indoor places increase the risk of transmission.

COVID-19 primarily affects the respiratory system, especially the lungs, but severe cases can also damage other organs such as the heart, brain, kidneys, and blood vessels. Symptoms vary widely from person to person. Some individuals experience no symptoms at all, while others develop mild to moderate symptoms such as fever, dry cough, sore throat, body aches, headache, fatigue, and loss of taste or smell. In severe cases, patients may suffer from pneumonia, difficulty breathing, respiratory failure, blood clots, and even death.

A significant number of patients experience long-term health problems after recovery, known as Long COVID. These may include chronic fatigue, memory and concentration problems, shortness of breath, chest pain, and mental health issues. The COVID-19 pandemic caused massive disruption worldwide, overwhelming healthcare systems and severely affecting economies, education, and social life.

Transmission

COVID-19 spreads primarily via respiratory droplets and aerosols produced when infected people breathe, talk, sing, cough, or sneeze. Transmission risk is highest in crowded and poorly ventilated indoor settings. Fomite transmission (via surfaces) is possible but plays a smaller role compared with aerosols.

Symptoms

Symptoms range from none (asymptomatic) to severe respiratory illness. Common symptoms include fever, dry cough, sore throat, fatigue, body aches, headache, loss of taste or smell, and shortness of breath. Severe complications include pneumonia, acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS), thrombotic events, multisystem inflammatory syndromes, and multi-organ failure.

Diagnosis & Treatment

Diagnosis is made by molecular testing (RT-PCR) or rapid antigen tests. Treatment depends on severity: outpatient care focuses on symptom relief and monitoring, while hospitalized patients may receive oxygen therapy, corticosteroids (for severe inflammation), antiviral drugs (e.g., remdesivir where indicated), and supportive intensive care. Monoclonal antibodies and antiviral agents are used selectively for high-risk patients.

Prevention

Prevention strategies include vaccination, masking in high-risk settings, improving ventilation, regular hand hygiene, testing and isolation, and avoiding crowded indoor events during surges. Vaccination remains the cornerstone for preventing severe disease; booster doses improve protection against emerging variants.

LATEST UPDATE

Although new variants continue to emerge, updated vaccines and improved outpatient and inpatient treatments have greatly reduced severe illness, hospitalizations, and deaths in many countries. Surveillance and vaccine updates continue to adapt to evolving variants.

Global Statistics & Impact

Total Global Cases: 770+ million confirmed cases

COVID-19 spread worldwide after first being detected in 2019 and became a global pandemic. It affected almost every country, disrupted daily life, healthcare systems, education, and economies on a massive scale. The virus had multiple variants, which increased transmission over time.
